From Sheep to Loom

How to take wool from a sheep's back all the way to a woven blanket.

  1. Pick a wool type.
  2. Sheer the sheep.
  3. Prepare the wool.
  4. Spin the yarn.
  5. Set up the loom.
  6. Weave.
  7. Finishing & fulling.

Pick a wool type.

There are a variety of different types of animals that wool can come from. Each type has propeties that make it appropriate for different uses. These are some example of my favorite types but it is far from an exahustive list.

  • Alpaca

  • Musk Ox

  • Merino Sheep

Sheer the sheep.

With a few exceptions, wool is aquired by sheering the animal. (A few, such as camels and musk oxen are combed.)

Prepare the wool.

Once it is off of the sheep, there are several steps to prepare the raw wool so it will be ready to spin.

  1. Wash the fleace:
    1. Use sythrapol or shampoo.
    2. Try to minimize agitation by filling the tub with water and then submerging the wool gently.
    3. Soak in suds for one or more hours.
    4. Use a sive or drain the water around the wool.
    5. Allow the wool to dry on a flat surface, ideally outside in the sun. (It will smell like poop.)
  2. Pluck the wool: After washing the wool and allowing it to dry, it will be matted and dense. Methodically pluck the wool to loosen it up.
